Nutrition Guide
"You cannot out-train a poor diet. Nutrition and exercise work together — both are non-negotiable."

🥩 Protein

Essential for muscle repair. Aim for adequate daily intake spread across meals.

  • Target: 1.6–2g per kg bodyweight
  • Spread across 3–4 meals per day
  • Chicken, eggs, fish, legumes, tofu
  • Greek yogurt, cottage cheese
  • Protein shakes to supplement gaps

💧 Hydration

Water is the most underrated performance and recovery tool.

  • Minimum 2.5–3 litres per day
  • Add 500ml per hour of exercise
  • Drink before you feel thirsty
  • Electrolytes on heavy training days
  • First glass every morning on waking

😴 Sleep

Muscle is built during sleep. Growth hormone peaks in deep sleep.

  • Aim for 7–9 hours nightly
  • Consistent sleep/wake schedule
  • Avoid screens 1 hour before bed
  • Cool, dark bedroom environment
  • Avoid large meals within 2 hrs of bed

🥗 Whole Foods

Base your diet on minimally processed, nutrient-dense whole foods.

  • Vegetables: fill half your plate
  • Fruits: 2–3 servings daily
  • Complex carbs: oats, rice, sweet potato
  • Healthy fats: avocado, nuts, olive oil
  • Minimise ultra-processed foods

🍽️ Meal Timing

When you eat matters, especially around training.

  • Pre-workout: light meal 60–90 min before
  • Post-workout: protein + carbs within 60 min
  • 3 main meals + 1–2 snacks daily
  • Don't skip breakfast on training days
  • Eat slowly and without distraction

🧾 Portion Guide

Use your hand as a portion tool — no scales needed.

  • Protein: 1 palm per meal
  • Carbs: 1 cupped handful per meal
  • Vegetables: 2 fistfuls per meal
  • Fats: 1 thumb per meal
  • Adjust based on hunger and results

Coaching Principles

Tip 1 — Consistency Over Intensity

Showing up every day at 70% beats showing up twice a week at 100%. The most powerful training variable is regularity. Build the habit first; intensity comes naturally with time.

Tip 2 — Track Everything

What gets measured gets managed. Log your workouts, measurements, and how you feel. Patterns in your data reveal what's working and what to adjust.

Tip 3 — Sleep Is Training

You don't build muscle in the gym — you break it down. Muscle is built during recovery and sleep. 7–9 hours of quality sleep is one of the highest-leverage actions you can take.

Tip 4 — Progress, Not Perfection

Missing one workout doesn't ruin your progress. Missing ten in a row does. If you miss a session, simply continue the next day. The goal is a long-term habit, not a perfect record.

Tip 5 — Identity-Based Habits

Instead of "I want to lose fat," say "I am someone who exercises daily." When your identity is tied to the behaviour, motivation becomes secondary — you act because of who you are.

Tip 6 — Nutrition Compounds

A single good meal changes nothing. A single bad meal changes nothing. But 180 consistent days of high-quality nutrition stacked on your training compounds into extraordinary results.
